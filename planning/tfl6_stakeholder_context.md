# TFL 6 / NICF Stakeholder Context

## Purpose

This note records stakeholder framing that should shape Phase 3 model-design
choices. It is not a legal, consultation, or negotiation record. It is a
teaching-model design lens for cedar, embedded NICF/K3Z identity, expansion
candidate areas, accounts, targets, reports, and scenario interpretation.

## Stakeholder Setting

The TFL 6 model area is larger than the original NICF/K3Z teaching context.
The embedded NICF/K3Z core and candidate expansion areas sit inside a larger
TFL 6 tenure context managed by Western Forest Products (WFP).

For the teaching instance, assume:

- NICF board members and local stakeholders are interested in cedar outcomes,
  cultural cedar visibility, community-forest continuity, and expansion
  opportunities.
- WFP may be sympathetic to NICF interests, but it is also a for-profit company
  with its own board, shareholders, mills, fibre supply commitments, and forest
  products supply-chain assets.
- WFP can reasonably be expected to push back on NICF-preferred scenarios that
  materially reduce WFP fibre supply volume, reduce delivered fibre value, or
  increase delivered unit cost in the broader TFL 6 context.

## Modeling Implications

The model should make tradeoffs visible instead of hiding them in assumptions.
The student-facing purpose is to let teams explore tensions among stakeholder
perspectives, not to optimize for one preferred viewpoint. Scenario comparisons
should use a range of output KPIs that proxy for values different stakeholders
care about, while recognizing that not every stakeholder agrees that every KPI
has the same importance or even the same sign.

Design requirements:

- Keep K3Z/NICF core, expansion candidates, TFL 6 remainder, and whole-TFL
  identities separately reportable.
- Track cedar availability, cedar harvest, and cedar reserve outcomes in both
  NICF/K3Z and broader TFL 6 contexts.
- Report expansion scenario effects on whole-TFL harvest volume, product value
  proxies, operability, and treatment cost or delivered-cost proxies where
  available.
- Preserve WFP-facing fibre-supply signals alongside NICF-facing community,
  cedar, and expansion signals.
- Preserve student-facing KPI families for multiple perspectives, including
  fibre supply, product-value proxies, delivered-cost proxies, cedar/cultural
  cedar signals, community-forest expansion opportunity, treatment opportunity,
  reserve/retention outcomes, and spatial distribution of effects.
- Treat conflict between NICF goals and WFP fibre-supply/value/cost constraints
  as a teaching feature, not a nuisance to average away.

## Patchworks-Facing Requirements

Phase 4 and later runtime surfaces should support:

- group accounts for NICF/K3Z core, expansion candidates, rejected candidates,
  TFL 6 remainder, and whole TFL 6;
- matching targets that compare NICF-preferred outcomes against whole-TFL and
  WFP-facing fibre-supply outcomes;
- reports that show whether expansion or cedar-reserve scenarios shift area,
  volume, product mix, or cost proxies away from the TFL 6 remainder;
- KPI tables that let students compare stakeholder-style perspectives without
  implying that all perspectives share the same objective function;
- scenario toggles that let students test NICF expansion/cedar assumptions
  without changing base AU identities; and
- narrative documentation that explains the likely governance tension between
  NICF objectives and WFP supply-chain constraints.

## Scope Boundary

This note does not decide which stakeholder position is correct. It records
that the model should expose the tradeoff space clearly enough for FRST 558
teaching, scenario comparison, and stakeholder-style discussion.
